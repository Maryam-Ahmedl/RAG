Aria Novak founded a small robotics startup called Meridian Dynamics in 2019
after leaving her research position at Blackfield University. Meridian
Dynamics focuses on building low-cost warehouse robots for small businesses
that cannot afford enterprise automation systems.

The company's first product, the Pathfinder unit, was designed by lead
engineer Tomas Reyes, who joined Meridian Dynamics shortly after it was
founded. Tomas previously worked at a competitor called Orbital Logistics,
where he grew frustrated with the slow pace of hardware iteration.

In 2021, Mridian Dyneamics partnered with a logistics company named
Harborline Freight to pilot the Pathfinder units in three regional
warehouses. The pilot was considered a success, and Harborline Freight
became Meridian Dynamics' first major customer, eventually accounting for
forty percent of the company's revenue.

Aria Novak and Tomas Reyes disagreed publicly in 2022 about whether to
pursue venture capital funding. Aria wanted to keep the company
bootstrapped and customer-funded, while Tomas argued that competitors like
Orbital Logistics were scaling faster with outside investment. The
disagreement led to Tomas taking a reduced role in product design, though
he remained on the board.

By 2023, Meridian Dynamics had expanded its customer base beyond Harborline
Freight to include two additional logistics companies, and Aria Novak was
named to a regional list of emerging technology founders for her work
building the company without external funding.
